Diver work

Project Description
With the rapid development of the global maritime transportation industry, the number and density of ship transportation at sea have been increasing, and the accompanying problems brought by ships to the environment have become more and more complicated, while the probability of serious maritime accidents and major oil spill pollution risks has also increased significantly. The number of shipwrecks in the world with potential pollution risk is 8596, including 1583 oil tankers and 6986 non-oil tankers, the total amount of oil not leaked and residual in these shipwrecks has reached 2.5 million tons ~ 20.4 million tons, if we do not take timely and effective measures for the rapid recovery of the residual oil stored in these shipwrecks, the potential pollution is a serious threat to the safety of the marine environment. The main task of this project is to address the rapid recovery of oil from shipwrecks, and the topic is divided into five parts: identification of the opening point, connection of the opening equipment, electronic control and automation equipment, robotic arm action planning, analysis, flow field simulation, shape design and robotic arm end-effector. The significance of this project is to make the recovery of crude oil from sunken ships more convenient, fast and safe at sea, which greatly increases the mobility of the ship and the safety of the work of recovering oil from sunken ships. At the same time, with the help of robotic arm to operate the drilling device and the wreck oil recovery valve docking and disengagement, to reduce the safety of personnel to go down to the depths of the sea to work, in order to better realize the rapid and effective recovery of the wreck oil treatment laid the foundation.
